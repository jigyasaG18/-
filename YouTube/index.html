What is Misarticulation?

Misarticulation refers to difficulty pronouncing certain sounds correctly. Children may substitute, omit, or distort sounds, which can impact their communication skills.

Common Types of Misarticulation:

  • Substitution: Replacing one sound with another (e.g., "wabbit" for "rabbit").
  • Omission: Leaving out sounds (e.g., "ca" instead of "cat").
  • Distortion: Producing a sound incorrectly (e.g., a slushy or unclear sound).

Speech therapy can help children improve their articulation skills through fun activities and exercises, making it enjoyable for them!
